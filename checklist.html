01

Register the death

Contact your local register office
Register within 5 days (England, Wales, NI) or 8 days (Scotland).
Obtain death certificates
Order at least 4–6 certified copies — banks and insurers each need one.
02

Use Tell Us Once

Notify government services via Tell Us Once
Covers DWP, HMRC, DVLA, Passport Office, and local council in one step.
03

Inform key organisations

Notify banks and building societies
Visit in branch or write — bring a certified death certificate.
Contact insurance providers
Life, home, car, and travel insurance.
Notify employer and pension providers
Check for a death-in-service benefit with the employer.
04

Manage finances

Request account details and balances
You will need this for probate if applicable.
Cancel unnecessary direct debits and payments
Check 2–3 months of bank statements to find all subscriptions.
05

Handle bills and utilities

Take meter readings and notify utility providers
Gas, electricity, and water. Take readings immediately to fix the final bill date.
Notify the council about council tax
The property may be exempt while empty during probate.
06

Check if probate applies

Locate the will and identify the executor
Check with solicitors, banks, or personal papers.
Check whether a Grant of Probate is needed
Usually required for solely owned property, large accounts, or investments.
